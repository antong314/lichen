# Description

The reframe round. The prompt explicitly invites tension into the answer — something that could be read negatively, but isn't. Pattern-level answers are welcome here. Groundedness caps the score: a beautifully shaped reframe without real substance is the failure mode.

# User Prompts

## used_to_frustrate
Name something your partner does that used to frustrate you, or that you didn't understand at first, that you've come to admire.

## seems_like_flaw
Name something about your partner that someone outside your relationship might see as a flaw, but that you see as a strength.

## annoying_then_endearing
Name a habit of your partner's that you'd be sad to lose, even though it sometimes annoys you.

## grew_into
Name something about your partner you didn't appreciate when you first got together, that you've grown into appreciating.

## old_argument
Name something you used to argue about that you've stopped arguing about — not because you gave up but because you understood it differently.

## hidden_in_difficulty
Name something difficult about your partner that you'd defend to anyone.

# Scoring Instructions

Apply the base rubric. For this round type:

- **Generosity of frame** is heavy. The hinge sentence — the moment the reframe lands — should be visible. Look for the move from naming a difficulty to resolving it into a strength.
- **Groundedness** is heavy and *caps* generosity of frame. If the reframe sounds elegant but doesn't have texture (incidental detail, small concessions, evidence of having actually wrestled with it), the answer is performance and should not exceed 2 hearts no matter how shapely.
- **Specificity** is medium. Pattern-level answers are appropriate here; do not penalize the absence of a single moment. But a specific incident that anchors the reframe is a meaningful boost.
- **Non-obviousness** is medium. Reward unusual reframes; the obvious "she's stubborn but it makes her loyal" type answer is mid.

The single biggest failure mode in this round is the eloquent-but-hollow answer. Be skeptical of beautiful sentences without grit.
