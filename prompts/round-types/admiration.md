# Description

The core round type. The player names something concrete and admirable about their partner. Scoring rewards specificity and groundedness most; generosity-of-frame is a bonus when it appears organically.

# User Prompts

## small_unnoticed
Name a small thing your partner did in the last week that most people wouldn't have noticed, but you did.

## nobody_else_sees
Name something your partner does that nobody outside your household would ever see.

## when_no_one_watching
Name something your partner does when they think no one is watching that you admire.

## micro_gesture
Name a specific gesture, phrase, or habit of your partner's that you find unreasonably good.

## quiet_competence
Name a quiet competence of your partner's — something they do well that they don't make a thing of.

## stranger_surprise
Name something a stranger would be surprised to learn about your partner — something you only know because you live with them.

## defines_relationship
Name a small, recurring thing between you that secretly defines your relationship — and what your partner contributes to it.

## unspoken_kindness
Name something kind your partner did recently that they didn't name as kindness.

# Scoring Instructions

Apply the base rubric. For this round type:

- **Specificity** is heavy. A concrete moment, gesture, or named detail should push toward 3 hearts. Abstract adjectives without anchoring detail cap at 1–2.
- **Groundedness** is heavy. Look for incidental texture — the small unrequested details that invented answers don't have.
- **Non-obviousness** is medium. The "small thing nobody noticed" framing already invites this; reward answers that go past the first thought.
- **Noticing** is medium. The receiver's tap will adjust this later; score what's plausible from the answer alone.
- **Generosity of frame** is a bonus. If it appears organically — naming a tension and resolving it — add weight. Do not penalize answers that don't reach for it.

If an answer is short but specific and grounded, score it generously. The game does not reward effort or word count.
